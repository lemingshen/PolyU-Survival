# Transportation

Hong Kong boasts one of the world's most efficient, reliable, and affordable public transportation systems. As a new student, understanding how to navigate the city is key to exploring all it has to offer. This guide covers the primary modes of transport you'll be using.

## The Essential: Octopus Card (八達通)

Before diving into the different transport options, the first thing you need is an **Octopus Card**.

It's a rechargeable contactless smart card used for almost all public transport, as well as for purchases at convenience stores, supermarkets, fast-food restaurants, and more.

*   **Where to get it**: You can buy one at any MTR station's customer service centre.
*   **Student Status**: Full-time students are eligible to apply for a **Personalised Octopus with "Student Status"**, which offers concessionary fares on the MTR. You can apply for this through the university after you've registered.

## MTR (Mass Transit Railway / 港鐵)

The MTR is the backbone of Hong Kong's transport network. It's a clean, fast, and air-conditioned subway system that covers all major districts in the city. The nearest MTR station to PolyU is **Hung Hom Station (紅磡站)**, which is directly connected to the campus.

*   **App**: Download the **MTR Mobile** app to plan your journeys, check fares, and get real-time train information.
*   **Tip**: Trains can be very crowded during rush hours (8:00-9:30 AM and 6:00-7:30 PM).

## Buses (巴士)

Hong Kong's iconic double-decker buses cover almost the entire city, including many places the MTR doesn't reach, such as the southern side of Hong Kong Island and remote country parks.

*   **How to Ride**: Board at the front and pay with your Octopus card or exact cash (no change is given). The stop name is announced and displayed in both English and Chinese. Press the red bell button when your stop is next.
*   **Apps**: Use apps like **Citybus** and **KMB** to check routes and estimated arrival times.

## Minibuses (小巴)

Minibuses are smaller buses that carry up to 19 people. They are a fast and efficient way to travel for shorter distances and can be a bit more adventurous.

*   **Green Minibuses (GMBs)**: Operate on fixed routes with fixed fares. You pay as you board.
*   **Red Minibuses (RMBs)**: Have more flexible routes and fares. You usually pay as you get off. You'll need to call out to the driver when you want to stop. This can be challenging if you don't speak Cantonese, but it's a true local experience!

## Trams (電車)

Affectionately known as "ding-dings," these historic double-decker trams run exclusively on Hong Kong Island, from Kennedy Town to Shau Kei Wan.

*   **How to Ride**: Enter at the back and go to the upper deck for the best views. The fare is a flat, cheap rate regardless of distance. Tap your Octopus card or pay the exact fare when you get off at the front.
*   **Tip**: Trams are not the fastest way to travel, but they are perfect for sightseeing and experiencing a piece of Hong Kong's history.

## Ferries (渡輪)

Ferries are essential for crossing Victoria Harbour and traveling to the outlying islands.

*   **Star Ferry**: The iconic Star Ferry provides a cheap and scenic crossing between Tsim Sha Tsui and Central/Wan Chai. It's a must-do for any newcomer.
*   **Outlying Islands**: Ferries from the Central Ferry Piers will take you to popular destinations like Lamma Island, Cheung Chau, and Lantau Island (Mui Wo).

## Taxis (的士)

Taxis are plentiful and relatively cheap compared to other major cities. They are a convenient option for direct routes, late-night travel, or when traveling in a group.

*   **Types**:
    *   **Red Taxis**: Urban taxis that serve most of Hong Kong.
    *   **Green Taxis**: Serve the New Territories.
    *   **Blue Taxis**: Serve Lantau Island.
*   **Payment**: Most taxis are cash-only, so make sure you have some on hand. Some may accept Octopus or other mobile payments, but always ask first.
*   **Tip**: It's helpful to have your destination address written in Chinese to show the driver.
